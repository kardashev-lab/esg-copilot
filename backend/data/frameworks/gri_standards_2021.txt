GRI Standards 2021 - Complete Framework

GRI 1: FOUNDATION
The GRI Standards are designed to be used by organizations to report about their impacts on the economy, environment, and people. The Standards are structured as a set of interrelated, modular standards.

GRI 2: GENERAL DISCLOSURES

GRI 2-1: Organizational details
The organization shall report the following information:
a) the organization's name;
b) a description of the organization's activities and primary brands, products, and services;
c) the location of the organization's headquarters;
d) the number of countries where the organization operates;
e) the nature of ownership and legal form;
f) the markets served;
g) the scale of the organization;
h) information about employees and other workers;
i) supply chain information;
j) significant changes to the organization and its supply chain;
k) the precautionary approach or principle;
l) externally developed economic, environmental, and social charters, principles, or other initiatives;
m) membership of associations;
n) a statement from the most senior decision-maker of the organization about the relevance of sustainability to the organization and the organization's strategy for addressing sustainability.

GRI 2-2: Entities included in the organization's sustainability reporting
The organization shall report the following information:
a) a list of all entities included in the organization's consolidated financial statements or equivalent documents;
b) a list of all entities not included in the organization's consolidated financial statements or equivalent documents that are included in the organization's sustainability reporting;
c) an explanation of the reasons for any entity being included in the organization's sustainability reporting but not in the organization's consolidated financial statements or equivalent documents;
d) an explanation of the reasons for any entity being included in the organization's consolidated financial statements or equivalent documents but not in the organization's sustainability reporting.

GRI 2-3: Reporting period, frequency and contact point
The organization shall report the following information:
a) the reporting period for the information provided;
b) the date of the most recent previous report;
c) the reporting cycle;
d) the contact point for questions regarding the report or its contents.

GRI 2-4: Restatements of information
The organization shall report the following information:
a) the reason for any restatements of information provided in previous reports;
b) the nature of the restatements;
c) the impact of the restatements on the information provided in the current report.

GRI 2-5: External assurance
The organization shall report the following information:
a) the approach to seeking external assurance for the report;
b) the relationship between the organization and the assurance providers;
c) whether the highest governance body or any senior executives are involved in seeking external assurance;
d) the scope and basis of any external assurance provided;
e) whether the report has been prepared in accordance with the GRI Standards.

GRI 3: MATERIAL TOPICS

GRI 3-1: Process to determine material topics
The organization shall report the following information:
a) a description of the process for determining material topics;
b) a description of the process for prioritizing material topics;
c) a description of how the organization has applied the principle of materiality in determining the content of the report.

GRI 3-2: List of material topics
The organization shall report the following information:
a) a list of the material topics identified in the process for determining material topics;
b) for each material topic, a description of why the topic is material;
c) for each material topic, a description of the organization's approach to managing the topic.

GRI 3-3: Management of material topics
The organization shall report the following information:
a) for each material topic, a description of how the organization manages the topic;
b) for each material topic, a description of the organization's approach to stakeholder engagement;
c) for each material topic, a description of the organization's approach to due diligence.
